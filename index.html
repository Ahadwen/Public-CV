Philosophy

How I think about product

"I want a pragmatic setting that values real work over the performance of productivity."

On roadmaps

Stakeholder excitement is not proof of concept

A roadmap that absorbs every excited request isn't a strategy, it's a list. The hard work is defending what doesn't make the cut — and building the case for it properly.

On platforms

Speed is a product decision, not just an engineering one

Getting from 5 to 20 partnerships a year at Alteos wasn't a headcount story — it was a platform architecture story. Product decisions that enable speed compound. The ones that don't eventually stop you.

On teams

Understand the problem and solution space deeply — not just vibes

Vision without context doesn't scale. I stay close to the technical and user reality — not to micromanage, but because context is what makes real autonomy possible.

On ambiguity

Disruption is a working environment, not an excuse

I don't need a clear brief to be effective. The plan changing is not a failure state — it's just Tuesday.
